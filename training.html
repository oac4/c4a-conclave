Period 1
9:00-9:45am
Digging through the Archives [OA History]
From the very first ceremony at Treasure Island to the very recent 100th anniversary NOAC, the OA is full of incredible and interesting history. Learn about the progression of ceremonies, different patch and sash designs, how the OA became recognized by the BSA, and much, much more! This training is suggested for new Arrowmen and anyone interested in Scouting history.
Training the New Recruits [Ordeal Planning]
The Ordeal weekend is the first event for new Arrowmen and the most memorable. Lucky for you, we're here to help! This session includes, but is not limited to, best practices for finding elangomats, food, timing of ceremonies/service, different types of service, and much more! This training is suggested for lodge leadership and anyone interested in the Ordeal process.
Battle Buddies [Elangomats]
There is no person more important in the Ordeal process than the elangomat. Learn from past elangomats and elangomat chairmen about the ins and outs of the job. This would include, how to make lasting memories for the candidates, how to help Arrowmen return to future events, and the "after ordeal" obligations of an elangomat. This training is recommended for aspiring and elangomats and lodge leadership.
New Software [Lodgemaster]
This session is designed for the lodge advisor of each lodge, or their designee, and one additional Arrowman. With the changes coming to the Lodgemaster program, there will be multiple things to discuss to guarantee the success of each lodge and their Lodgemaster usage. Please, only register for this training if you have specific instructions from your lodge adviser.
Friends in the Right Places [Social Media & Marketing]
Let's be honest, how many of us are currently signed into Facebook as we speak? Social Media has taken our nation by storm and is a great way to promote your chapter, lodge, and the Order in general. In this session, you will learn from the best tweeters, pokers, and followers about different ways to make your lodge stand out on the Social Media scene. This session is recommended for all Arrowmen.
Period 2
10:00-10:45am
Show us Your Best
Do you think you have an innovative, effective idea that will change the Order of the Arrow for the better!? Then come and compete against other lodges in a "Shark Tank" style interview. Each lodge will have five minutes to present their new initiative in front of a panel composed of various OA leadership. Even if you are not on your lodge's presentation team, you may still come and be a part of the audience! This training is recommended for all Arrowmen, especially lodge leadership.
Rest and Relaxation [Fellowship Planning]
Arrowmen come for the Ordeal, but stay for the Fellowships. In this training, you will learn the most important steps to make your Fellowships memorable, exciting, and fun so that your Arrowmen will want to keep coming back for more. Topics will include themes, service projects, fun events, food, and more. This training is recommended for lodge leadership or anyone interested in running a Fellowship.
Earning your Sash [Ordeal Ceremony]
"Awake, my friends." This session will take an in depth look at the Ordeal Ceremony and how to leave a lasting impression for the new Arrowmen. This will not be a ceremonial competition, but rather a discussion on the best ceremonial practices from each lodge, including regalia, props, and circle set up. This training is recommended for all ceremonialists, inductions chairmen, or anyone interested in props and grounds.
New Insignia [Patch Design]
If there is one thing every Arrowman has in common, it is the love of patches. Learn from the best artists in the section about the best ways to promote your event, lodge, and the OA through the many patches you may design. This training is recommended for patch traders and designers alike.

Knowing your Gadgets [Technology in the OA]
You can't escape technology in the 21st century, so learn to embrace it! Join the tech gurus of the section as they present new and exciting ways to reinvigorate your lodge through the use of technology. This training is recommended for lodge leadership.
Moving on Up [Brotherhood Ceremony]
Learn the best ways to help your fellow brothers seal their membership in the Order. This session will discuss the best practices from each lodge while performing the Brotherhood Ceremony. Learn from past All-Stars the best ways to read and project the script as well as other topics, such as regalia and circle set up. This training is recommended for Brotherhood ceremonialists. You must be a Brotherhood or Vigil Honor member to attend.
